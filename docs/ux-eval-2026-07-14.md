# UI/UX Walkthrough — 2026-07-14

Evaluation of the nine demo screenshots (`docs/guide/img/01-…09-*.png`) through
two lenses: an end user (ADHD-first usability) and a clinician (safety,
screening presentation, privacy claims). Findings only — nothing here is
implemented. Items worth building should be folded into the PLAN.md tier list.

**Scope caveat:** this covers what the screenshots show. Onboarding, Reconfig,
directive detail sheets, and anything below the fold (Backups, Trauma Team
export UI, possible Telemetry charts) were not evaluated.

---

## The two findings that matter most

### 1. Crash kit reachability (clinician — highest priority)

The crash kit itself is excellent: warm copy ("You're here. Right move."),
no wrong door, call *and* text 988, findahelpline.com for non-US, explicit
"nothing is logged" statement. Best-in-class for a consumer app.

The problem is getting to it. The "Open crash kit" button lives below the fold
on Directives and is **not in the navigation menu**. A person in crisis has
degraded executive function — every scroll and every screen between them and
the kit is attrition. Clinical standard for crisis resources: reachable from
any screen, in a consistent location, without scrolling.

**Recommend:** a persistent small affordance (header icon or fixed menu entry,
same spot on every screen) + add it to the nav drawer.

### 2. PHQ-9 item 9 gating (clinician — the app's biggest clinical gap)

PHQ-9 question 9 is the self-harm item. Any consumer app administering the
PHQ-9 is expected to respond immediately when item 9 is endorsed (>0):
surface the crash kit / 988 right then, in the results flow — not just show
a score. This is the single highest-value clinical feature the app could add,
it's pillar-compatible (local-only, no logging of the response), and it's
cheap: one conditional in the screener results view.

Same logic applies to a total score in the moderately-severe/severe range
(≥15): pair the score with a gentle "worth showing your care team" nudge and
a crash-kit link, never alarm language.

## Global findings (all screens)

| # | Finding | Lens | Severity |
|---|---------|------|----------|
| G1 | **Install banner eats ~15% of every screen.** It appears on all nine shots. If it isn't dismissible-and-remembered, make it so; either way, dismiss it in the screenshot pipeline so docs images show the real app. | user | med |
| G2 | **Everything is behind the hamburger.** For a 1–2-tap doctrine app, the daily loop (Directives ↔ Training ↔ Telemetry) costs 2 taps + a drawer scan each switch. A bottom tab bar (3–4 items: Directives / Grind / Telemetry, maybe Crash Kit) is the standard mobile fix and would be the biggest single flow improvement. | user | high |
| G3 | **"LINKED" chip is ambiguous.** In a no-account app, "LINKED" reads like "linked to a server/account" — the opposite of the privacy story. The established lexicon already has the right pair: ON-GRID / OFF-GRID. | user | low |
| G4 | **Unexplained iconography.** ⚡21d and ▣3 on Directives have no affordance to learn what they mean (Telemetry's shields tile has an ⓘ; the home screen, where they matter, doesn't). One tap-to-explain covers it. | user | med |

## Per-screen findings

### 01 — Directives
- **Strong:** big tap targets, per-habit XP, one-tap check, hydration with
  presets *and* an undo (−8 oz) — genuinely forgiving design.
- "⊞ Reconfig" floats alone with no context; unclear what it does or that
  it's tappable.
- Crash kit entry is below the fold here (see finding #1).

### 02 — Training
- **Strong:** free-text + format chips + "add set (repeats last)" is a fast
  path; the `//` footer honestly explains the suggestion list and #tags.
- History rows don't look tappable — can the user see the sets from
  "Push day · 48 min"? If yes, add an affordance; if no, that's the feature.
- **Feature idea:** "Repeat last workout" one-tap. The demo data itself shows
  Push day recurring; pre-filling last time's exercises/weights is the
  highest-value ADHD fast path on this screen.

### 03 — Bio
- **Copy bug:** the Resting HR input placeholder shows "e.g. 120/80 w…" —
  a blood-pressure example in a bpm field. Wrong example, and it implies the
  field parses BP strings.
- **"PHQ-9 · LAST 5" is ambiguous** — last score 5? last 5 entries? 5 days
  ago? Spell it out ("last: 5/27").
- Weight shows a delta "(-0.6)". Minor, but weight deltas are eating-disorder
  adjacent; consider making the delta display opt-in per metric. The
  no-nagging cadence design is already the right call.
- **Strong (clinician):** the screener framing is exactly right — optional,
  no XP, no streaks, explained *why* ("so answers stay honest"), export
  framed as for your care team. A clinician would nod at this paragraph.
- Screener history shows one line ("2026-07-04 · PHQ-9 5/27 · mild") — see
  Telemetry note on trends.

### 04 — Feed
- **Strong:** "Quick session, no title" kills the cataloguing friction that
  makes ADHD users abandon reading trackers; one-time checkbox is a smart
  anti-clutter feature.
- Layout bug: the one-time checkbox floats detached above its label text.
- History note icon (📝) has no visible way to read the note.

### 05 — Goals
- **Strong:** "ahead/behind pace" framing (pace, not perfection) matches
  doctrine.
- The badge says *ahead* but not what pace needs — "~17 pages/day keeps you
  on pace" would make it actionable.
- The screen is 70% empty. Cheap wins for the space: last period's result
  ("last week: 128/120 ✓") or a forgiving weeks-met tally.

### 06 — Telemetry
- **Strong:** the plain-language System Report is the best screen in the app
  for both audiences — "3 shields banked against missed days" mentions misses
  without shame, and "the week had good frames" is the doctrine in one line.
- Naming collision: "vitals averaged 3.6/5" is the *mood* check-in, but Bio
  calls body metrics vitals. Pick one word per concept.
- **Feature idea (clinician-driven):** trend lines — mood over 30/90 days and
  PHQ-9/GAD-7 score over time. This is the #1 thing a care team wants to see,
  and it belongs both here and in the Trauma Team export. (If charts exist
  below the fold, extend the screenshot pipeline to capture them.)

### 07 — System
- Each habit row renders a second, unlabeled emoji after its name
  ("Touch grass — walk outside · 🧘"). It's the *area* icon, but unlabeled it
  reads as a rendering glitch — especially when it mismatches the habit icon.
  Label it or drop it.
- "OPEN CODEX" is unexplained flavor at a decision point. One clarifying
  subtitle word fixes it.
- edit/archive/delete inline with delete in red: good, assuming delete
  confirms (per doctrine it must).

### 08 — Menu
- Structure is clean; version + connection status at the bottom is a nice
  touch. GRIND grouping is fine *if* the drawer remembers its expanded state.
- **Crash kit is absent from the menu** — see finding #1.

### 09 — Crash kit
- Copy, ordering (self-help first, crisis line prominent), non-US fallback,
  and the no-logging statement are all right. Keep it exactly this tone.
- The overlay shows the gamified UI (hydration bar) behind it; a fuller
  scrim would reduce visual noise at the worst moment.
- Deliberately **not** recommended: journaling/logging inside the kit. The
  zero-logging property is the feature.

---

## Recommendations, ranked

1. **PHQ-9 item-9 (and score ≥15) → immediate, gentle crash-kit offer** in the
   screener results flow. Clinical table stakes; cheap; pillar-compatible.
2. **Persistent crash-kit access** — nav menu entry + consistent header/fixed
   affordance on every screen, above the fold.
3. **Bottom tab bar** for the daily loop (Directives / Grind / Telemetry).
4. **Mood + screener trend views** in Telemetry and the Trauma Team export.
5. **"Repeat last workout"** one-tap on Training.
6. Copy/polish batch: HR placeholder bug, "LAST 5" label, vitals naming
   collision, area-emoji rows in System, LINKED→ON-GRID wording, Reconfig and
   OPEN CODEX clarity, Feed checkbox alignment, tap-to-explain for ⚡/▣.
7. Install-banner dismissal persistence + hide it in the screenshot pipeline.
